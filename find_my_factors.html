Factors

Factors are positive integers (whole numbers) that can divide an integer exactly, i.e. with a remainder of zero. Factors always come in pairs, with the smallest number of factors a number can have being 2. When a number has only 2 factors, that means it's prime, only being able to be divided by 1 and itself.
